# TerpLoad
TerpLoad is our startup project idea. The goal is to help first year and/or CS/STEM students understand whether a planned semester schedule is manageable before fully committing.

Essentially, we want to be able to help these students understand whether they can handle their planned techincal course schedule and inform them of important considerations or details on the schedules.

## Persona
Our main specific target is a UMD CS/STEM student planning a required or workload heavu course combination(that they might not even realize), which includes students who are entering harder higher level major courses, balancing required classes, and also just unsure how they might need to divide their time because they don't know if their planned semester is manageable.


## Pain
Before a semester starts, students are unaware of what a combination of courses will be demanding of them. Usually, the problem doesn't come from a singular hard class but rather a combination of multiple classes together. This pain can come from projects, exams, homework, assignments, labs, or essays, or even a combination of all those for each class.

It is not to say that the student should avoid a class, as their specific combination of courses might be required to take together during a semester before moving on to the next or they do it out of choice according to how they plan to take courses. They need to know what they are walking into, what specific parts of the schedule could be risky, if the workload can stack badly, or if they can separate certain courses if possible. If that specific schedule is required and the student can't change it, then they would at least need to know how to prepare.

## The Hack
Currently the students solve this problem through sources like PlanetTerp, Reddit, friends who already took the course, any past GroupMe's, meetings with advisors. The problem is if none of that really helped they might even guess just based on professor ratings.

From our interviews, we noticed that most people said friends and students who alrady took the course were the most trusted source. However, the problem with that is that some of them could have different professors and different structure, and also they might not have struggled or had problems to begin with. This makes their advice maybe not 100% accurate and more incomplete. 

## User Validation 
We interviewed four sutdents who we discovered had experience planning difficult technical course combinations.

### what we noticed and patterns in interviews
* All of them said the problem happened to be a course combination issue, not just one hard class. They were all CS/STEM students, 2 of them rising seniors, one graduated student who spoke about their sophomore year undergrad schedule, and one graduate ML student.
* They relied heavily on friends who took the courses before, but also used PlanetTerp or Reddit.
* all agreed that the semester was harder than expected or difficult to judge beforehand.
* Main thing they said was wanting more info about project workload, time commitment, and exam difficulty.
