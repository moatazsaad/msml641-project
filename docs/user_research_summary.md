Main finding:
The problem is not only one hard class. The main pain is risky course combinations.

Evidence:
- Interview 1: Applied ML student had difficulty from MSML604, MSML605, MSML606 together.
- Interview 2: CS student had project-heavy CS courses plus exams/quizzes/essays from GenEds.
- Interview 3: CS + Econ student had CMSC216, CMSC250, organic chemistry, and biology together.
- Interview 4: CS student had CMSC330, CMSC351, and STAT400.

Current hacks:
Students use friends, PlanetTerp, Reddit, advisors and past GroupMe chats.

Missing information:
- project workload
- exam difficulty
- weekly time commitment
- professor differences
- course structure changes
- whether multiple hard classes stack badly

Product implication:
TerpLoad should explain workload risk, not just give a rating.
